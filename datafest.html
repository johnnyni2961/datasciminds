This year, MINDS is holding our 1st Annual MINDSet, a collection of events geared towards aspiring tomorrowâ€™s data scientists. As a part of MINDSet, we will be holding a MINDS DataFest. Our DataFest aims to empower students in pursuing data science through a series of free workshops, encouraging peer collaboration. The event will feature two workshop sessions, in which esteemed speakers will teach data science techniques including decision trees, regression, data scrubbing, t-tests, and neural-network machine learning. Following the workshops, participants will be divided into randomized breakout rooms to discuss the material and analyze provided COVID-19 datasets.This year, we will analyze this COVID-19 dataset.

All workshops will be in R, a software used in academia and industry for data science.

Participate in various workshops on statistical analysis and programming geared to multiple experience levels. Experts will teach coding techniques such as data scrubbing, t-tests, regression, decision trees, and neural networks. All workshops will be in R, a software used in academia and industry for data science.

Listen to a Plenary panel of statistical experts discuss their own findings on the same COVID-19 dataset.

Then, participate in break-out sessions in which the Plenary speakers will share and dialogue on how they developed their findings.
