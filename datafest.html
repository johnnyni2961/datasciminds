The MINDSet DataFest is a collaborative event focused on helping students from many different backgrounds to learn data science in an interactive way. This year, we will analyze this COVID-19 dataset.

Participate in various workshops on statistical analysis and programming geared to multiple experience levels. Experts will teach coding techniques such as data scrubbing, t-tests, regression, decision trees, and neural networks. All workshops will be in R, a software used in academia and industry for data science.

Listen to a Plenary panel of statistical experts discuss their own findings on the same COVID-19 dataset.

Then, participate in break-out sessions in which the Plenary speakers will share and dialogue on how they developed their findings.

  • 9 am- Welcome to the Collaborative Event
  • 9:30 am- Introduction to Data Set
  • 10 am- Student Teamwork: Explore/Discuss Data
  • 10:30 am- Workshop on Data Scrubbing
  • 11 am- Student Teamwork: Practice Data Scrubbing
  • 11:30 am- lunch
  • 12 pm- Workshop 1 on Data Analysis
  • 12:30 pm- Workshop 2 on Data Analysis
  • 1 pm- Student Teamwork: Practice Data Analysis
  • 1:30 pm- Students Present Findings
  • 2 pm- Plenary: Professionals Provide Short Presentations Highlighting their Findings on the Data
  • 3 pm- Breakout Rooms with Professionals
  • 4 pm- Closing
